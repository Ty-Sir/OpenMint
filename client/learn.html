A non-fungible token (NFT) is a unique digital item held on a blockchain where proof of ownership is easily shown. On OpenMint, NFTs are used to represent digital and physical artwork which can include but is not limited to exclusive access to redeem a physical product or high-res download. For more information about NFTs click here.

1) Click/tap CREATE in the navigation bar.

2) Connect your MetaMask wallet if you haven't already done so. (see below if in need of assistance)

3) Choose single 1-of-1, this is the ERC-721 token standard.

4) Upload the artwork. (if larger than 64 MB see Unlockable Content below)

5) Set a price if you want to sell the artwork. (this can always be changed later)

6) Choose whether you want to have the buyer receive exclusive unlockable content on purchase.
Note: THIS CANNOT BE SET OR CHANGED LATER

7) Choose your royalty percentage. This is the percentage of the sale price you will receive on every subsequent purchase of your artwork.

8) Choose a title for your artwork.
Note: THIS CANNOT BE CHANGED LATER

9) Choose a description for your artwork. (optional)
Note: THIS CANNOT BE CHANGED LATER

10) Add any additional info regarding the artwork or any unlockable content. (optional)
Note: THIS CAN BE SET OR CHANGED BY THE OWNER

11) Click the create button.

12) If going on sale click the 'Set Approval To Sell' button and confirm in MetaMask (this only needs to be done once), then click 'Upload and Mint' and confirm in MetaMask.

13) If the artwork is going on sale click 'Put On Sale' and then confirm in MetaMask.

At the current time, only MetaMask is allowed to interact with OpenMint. Use of any others is ill-advised until further notice.

Unlockable content is any information you want the owner of the NFT you make to have exclusive access to. This can be anything from a link to a high-res download to a password to a website to unlock a product. Your imagination is the limit. Just keep in mind that this information can only be set when creating the NFT and cannot be changed later.

A royalty is a certain percentage of the sale price that is automatically held in an escrow contract when a sale is successful and sits securely until withdrawn by the original creator. It is set during the creation process and cannot be changed due to the nature of a blockchain. At OpenMint we have a minimum of 1% and a maximum of 50% able to be set.

OpenMint uses the WBNB/BUSD pairing from 1inch to give a USD approximation. These prices are subject to change.

At OpenMint security is a top priority, which is why we have chosen to minimize any payment interceptions from hackers and do not send payments directly to an artist's wallet. When you have received a payment for a sale, go to your profile (top-right in the navigation bar) and you will see an available balance to withdraw under your bio. Secondary sale payments received (royalties) are also withdrawn the same way.

At OpenMint your artwork files are securely stored and pinned on IPFS through a gateway provided by Moralis.
